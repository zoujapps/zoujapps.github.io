🔐 Privacy Policy

GENIUS RUN — Last updated: July 18, 2026

Genius Run (referred to as "the App" or "We") is a minimalist endless platformer game for mobile devices. We take your privacy seriously. This page explains how we handle your data when you use the App.

📌 1. Data We Collect Directly

We do not collect any personal data directly such as your name, email address, phone number, or precise location.

The App does not require an account and never asks for any personally identifiable information.

📲 2. Automatically Collected Data (via Third-Party)

The App uses Google AdMob to serve interstitial (full-screen) advertisements. Google may automatically collect certain information to provide relevant ads, including:

  • Advertising ID — a resettable identifier provided by your device.
  • Device type, OS version, and model.
  • IP address (for general geographic analytics).
  • Ad interaction behavior (views, clicks, dismissals).

All such data is handled strictly in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy, available at: policies.google.com/privacy .

💾 3. Local Data Storage

Your game progress (best level, total gems, sound/haptic settings, and current level) is stored locally on your device only using SharedPreferences.

This data is never uploaded to our servers or any third-party servers. You can delete it at any time by clearing the App's storage or uninstalling the App.

👶 4. Children's Privacy (COPPA & GDPR-K)

This App is not intended for children under the age of 14. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone

In compliance with legal requirements, AdMob may serve non‑personalized ads to users under the age of consent where applicable.

🔗 5. Third-Party Links

The App may contain links to external sites (e.g., a "Rate" button leading to the Play Store). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external platforms. We encourage you to review their policies.

🛡️ 6. Data Security

Since we do not collect or store any personal data on our servers, the risk of your personal data being exposed is minimal. Any locally stored data is subject to the security measures of your own device (e.g., screen lock, encryption).
